Okonomiyaki has two major varieties, one from Osaka and one from Hiroshima, the latter of which is pictured. The difference between them lies in the way their ingredients are layered. Both can be considered a savory pancake.

Chūgoku Dessert

Chūgoku is known for Okayama prefecture's peaches! In the "Land of Sunshine" it is a popular family activity to go peach picking. A popular regional and seasonal dessert of the region is Hakuto jelly, a cloudlike peach jelly that melts in your mouth. It's also a popular summertime topping in fruit parfaits as pictured!

Sanuki Udon is a thick noodle dish with a distinctive square shape and flat edges. It is a specialty of the Shikoku region and popular in the Kagawa prefecture. It is produced using artisan techniques and noodle cutters.

Shikoku Dessert

Ehime is endearingly called "Kakigori Prefecture" since it is known for their high quality citrus and fruit which produces Kakigori syrup. Kakigori is shaved ice drizzled in flavored syrup and makes for tasty summertime fare. Lemon Kakigori homegrown in Matsuyoma City is pictured and looks very refreshing.

Miso Ramen is a popular soup base that helps bring a unique flavor. After selecting your choice of broth you add Awase, a mixed miso paste, which imparts a slight kick. It is one of Sapporo's specialties, their soup is kotteri, rich and oily, and often served with chili oil.

Hokkaido Food

Hokkaido Food sweet treat

Takoyaki are fried octopus dumplings that are commonly sold as street food or by festival vendors. It consists of flavored egg batter with tempura flakes, tenkasu, and octopus filling along with a layer of sauce and bonito flakes to top it off.

Kansai Food

Mitarashi dango is a popular street food that is made of grilled mochi covered in sweet and salty mitarashi sauce, a combination of soy sauce, sugar, starch, and water. It's origins lie in Kyoto's Shimagamo-jinja shrine's Mitarashi festival where it is traditionally served as 5 dangos on a skewer which represent the human body.
